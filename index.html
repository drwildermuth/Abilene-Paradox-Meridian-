Step 2 of 10

Case briefing

The Meridian AI Integration Initiative would expand a small pilot into a school-wide approach for AI use in design education. The committee is meeting before the proposal goes to the university senate.

After video

What is being proposed?

A phased rollout across studio courses. Faculty would add AI-supported activities for brainstorming, critique preparation, process documentation, and portfolio reflection. Students would receive common guidance on what counts as acceptable assistance versus misrepresentation of their own work.

What did the pilot show?

In a few courses, many students used the tools and asked for clearer expectations. Some faculty saw promise for experimentation and feedback. The pilot was small, so it did not settle every question about fairness, access, originality, or what “mastery” should mean in a design program.

What must the committee decide?

They are not writing the final policy today. They are deciding whether to recommend that Meridian move forward with the initiative, and what conditions should accompany that recommendation.

Your role

You are reviewing the meeting process. Notice how agreement forms, what people seem to assume about one another, and which concerns become easy or hard to say out loud.

After each meeting round, you may interview one person. You may also continue without interviewing, but skipped interviews cannot be recovered later.

Step 10 of 10

Confidential files and diagnosis

The Abilene Paradox happens when a group collectively supports a course of action that individual members privately do not want or do not fully support. The problem is not open conflict. The problem is misread agreement. Each person thinks others are more comfortable than they really are, so concerns stay unspoken.
